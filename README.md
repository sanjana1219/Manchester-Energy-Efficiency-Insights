# Manchester-Energy-Efficiency-Insights

Project Overview 

This project analyzes Energy Performance Certificate (EPC) data from Manchester (England and Wales) between 2014 and 2024 to understand patterns in residential building energy efficiency. The analysis aims to identify trends in energy performance ratings, CO₂ emissions, energy costs, and efficiency improvement potential.

The project combines SQL for data management and cleaning with Power BI for interactive data visualization, enabling insights into how building energy performance has evolved over time.


Objectives


•	Analyze residential energy efficiency trends from 2014–2024

•	Identify CO₂ emission patterns across properties

•	Evaluate energy cost distribution

•	Measure efficiency improvement potential

•	Provide insights to support sustainable housing and energy policies


Technologies Used


•	SQL – Database creation, data cleaning, and querying

•	Power BI – Interactive dashboard development and visualization

•	Data Analysis – Energy efficiency and environmental impact analysis


Methodology


1.	Imported EPC dataset into the SQL database.

2.	Performed data cleaning and transformation using SQL queries.

3.	Connected the processed dataset to Power BI.

4.	Created interactive dashboards to explore energy performance indicators.

5.	Analyzed patterns related to CO₂ emissions, energy efficiency, and potential energy savings.
	
  
Key Dashboard Visualizations


The Power BI dashboard includes:

•	CO₂ vs Energy Efficiency Density View

•	Energy Cost Distribution

•	Efficiency Improvement Potential

•	Energy Flow Overview

•	Current vs Potential Energy Comparison

•	Efficiency Target Tracking

•	KPI Cards

o	Average Energy Cost Saving

o	CO₂ Reduction Potential

•	Interactive Filters (Slicers) for dynamic analysis


Key Insights


•	Identified trends in energy efficiency improvements across residential properties.

•	Highlighted potential CO₂ reduction opportunities.

•	Provided insights into energy cost savings and sustainability improvements.
